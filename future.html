What is a Quantitative Trader?

A Quantitative Trader is essentially a "financial engineer" who combines mathematics and data through programming to master the market.

Motivation

Why I'm Interested

I am interested in becoming a quantitative trader because I have been investing in stocks since I was 14 years old. This experience made me interested in numbers, market patterns, and data-based decision-making.

I have always enjoyed mathematics and problem-solving, which is one of the main reasons I am attracted to this career. I also find it exciting that quantitative trading requires quick and logical thinking. My current major in Artificial Intelligence is closely connected to this goal, because it helps me build skills in programming, data analysis, and analytical thinking. I believe this career suits both my interests and strengths.

Roadmap

Goals & Plans

Improve my programming, mathematics, and data analysis skills while continuing my studies at UTS. Learn more about financial markets and how data is used in trading decisions.

In the near future, I hope to gain practical experience through internships in finance, trading, or analytics-related roles. These short-term goals will help me build a strong foundation for my long-term career.

My long-term goal is to become a quantitative trader after graduation. I am especially interested in working at a trading company or financial firm where I can learn about markets, risk, and data-driven decision-making. In the long term, I hope to work in a professional trading environment where I can apply both my technical knowledge and analytical thinking.
