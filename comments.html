Behind the scenes

Comments
on Design

An overview of the technical structure, design choices, and accessibility considerations behind this website.

Technical

Overall Structure

The website is made up of four pages: Home (introduces who I am), Past (explains my background), Future (describes my career goals), and Comments (explains the design and structure). All pages share an external stylesheet (style.css) for consistent layout, colours, fonts, and spacing, and are connected through the same navigation menu.

Internal Structure

Common Page Structure

All pages follow a similar internal structure: a header with the page title, a navigation menu, the main content section, and a footer. Content is organised using paragraphs, headings, lists, tables, images, and div containers depending on the page's purpose. This repeated structure makes the website easier to understand and gives all pages a unified feel.

index.html

Designed as the home page. Main content includes introduction paragraphs, a travel section with a list and image, and a Favourite Things section.

past.html

Presents my educational background using a table with school logos, stages, periods, and past dreams. Includes a reflective paragraph below.

future.html

Organised into multiple content sections explaining my future career goal — what a quantitative trader is, why I'm interested, skills to develop, and long/short-term goals.

comments.html

An analytical page covering overall and internal structure, styles used, graphic design, alt text, accessibility, and how the site works without CSS.

Design

Graphic Design

Aesthetics

The overall graphic design focuses on a soft, personal, and consistent appearance. I wanted the website to feel warm and friendly rather than formal or complicated, so I used a simple multi-page layout and avoided too many decorative effects.

Colour

I mainly used pink and brown tones to create a warm and consistent theme. Pink is my favourite colour, so I wanted it reflected in the design. Lighter pink backgrounds make the pages feel soft and personal, while darker brown creates contrast for headings and important text.

Font / Typography

Page titles use a bold display-style font to stand out clearly. Body text uses a traditional serif font for readability. Bold text is used selectively with the span element to highlight important words.

Layout / Interactivity

A consistent top-down layout is used across all pages. Flexbox is used on the home page to place the travel list and image side by side. Hover effects on navigation links and background music add interactivity and a personal feel.

Accessibility

Alt Text & Accessibility

All images include descriptive alt text — for example, "A traveling picture" and "UTS logo" — so users who cannot see images can still understand the content. I used clear colour contrast between text and background (brown text on a light pink background) to make content easy to read.

Headings are large and clearly separated from body text, which helps users understand the structure. The navigation menu is simple, and body text is large enough to read comfortably with added spacing between sections. Standard HTML elements (headings, paragraphs, lists, tables) are used throughout so content remains understandable even without CSS.

Resilience

How the Site Works Without CSS

When CSS is turned off, the website loses its visual design but remains fully usable. The navigation menu still works, headings and paragraphs remain in order, and lists and tables still display information clearly. Alt text for images also helps users understand image content without the visual design.
